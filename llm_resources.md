# 10 Essential LLM Resources for Beginners (with CP background) – Bullet List with Time Estimates

**Note:**  
- **Consume** = time to read/watch/listen once.  
- **Internalize** = additional time to take notes, re‑explain, write small code snippets, or solve related small problems.  
- Order is from most beginner‑friendly to more advanced.

---

### 1. The Illustrated Transformer
- **Type:** Article (visual walkthrough)  
- **URL:** https://jalammar.github.io/illustrated-transformer/  
- **Strength:** Visual, intuitive explanation of Transformer architecture; perfect for CP mindset.  
- **Weakness:** No code, no training pipeline details.  
- **Time (consume):** 1.5 hours (reading and looking at diagrams).  
- **Time (internalize):** 2–3 hours (draw your own diagrams, explain attention to a friend, write a one‑page summary).

---

### 2. Attention in transformers, step‑by‑step by 3Blue1Brown
- **Type:** YouTube video series  
- **URL:** https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/attention  
- **Strength:** Step‑by‑step mathematical intuition; excellent for logical verification.  
- **Weakness:** Covers only attention, not full LLM pipeline.  
- **Time (consume):** 1 hour (all videos at normal speed).  
- **Time (internalize):** 2–4 hours (pause frequently, re‑watch sections, replicate small matrix calculations on paper).

---

### 3. “And that's how you use LLMs” by Andrej Karpathy
- **Type:** YouTube talk (≈1 hour)  
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EWvNQjAaOHw  
- **Strength:** Real‑world practitioner insights; covers workflows, context windows, tokenisation.  
- **Weakness:** More about effective use than building from scratch.  
- **Time (consume):** 1 hour.  
- **Time (internalize):** 2 hours (take notes on key workflows and limitations; think how they apply to your lab project).

---

### 4. Microsoft’s Generative AI for Beginners
- **Type:** Online course (21 lessons)  
- **URL:** https://microsoft.github.io/generative-ai-for-beginners/  
- **Strength:** Structured syllabus; covers prompting, RAG, fine‑tuning.  
- **Weakness:** Early lessons may be too basic for CS graduate.  
- **Time (consume):** 10–12 hours (skimming all lessons, doing quick reads).  
- **Time (internalize):** 15–20 hours (complete quizzes, code along with the examples, adapt one lesson to a small dataset).

---

### 5. Aalto University: Introduction to Large Language Models
- **Type:** University course (self‑paced)  
- **URL:** https://www.aalto.fi/en/lifewide-learning-courses-and-programmes/introduction-to-large-language-models  
- **Strength:** Formal academic overview; solidifies fundamentals.  
- **Weakness:** Theoretical, not hands‑on.  
- **Time (consume):** 6–8 hours (reading lecture notes and watching videos).  
- **Time (internalize):** 6–10 hours (write definitions of key terms, create a concept map, compare with the Illustrated Transformer).

---

### 6. The LLM Course by Maxime Labonne (mlabonne/llm‑course)
- **Type:** GitHub repository (code tutorials)  
- **URL:** https://github.com/mlabonne/llm-course  
- **Strength:** Code‑focused bootcamp; two parts (LLM Scientist + LLM Engineer).  
- **Weakness:** Assumes Python and basic ML knowledge.  
- **Time (consume):** 15–20 hours (reading all sections and understanding the code).  
- **Time (internalize):** 30–40 hours (run every notebook, modify hyperparameters, reproduce results on a small model).

---

### 7. “The LLM Stack” by Uli Hitzel
- **Type:** Book (PDF, free)  
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/uhitzel_long-live-print-so-excited-to-see-universitas-activity-7447835051037302784-uC25 (see post for PDF)  
- **Strength:** Explains six‑layer architecture of LLM applications.  
- **Weakness:** More about architecture than mathematical details.  
- **Time (consume):** 4–5 hours (reading the PDF once).  
- **Time (internalize):** 4–6 hours (draw your own stack diagram, map your planned research project onto the layers).

---

### 8. “Foundations of Large Language Models” by joeduffy
- **Type:** Book (PDF, curated paper collection)  
- **URL:** https://github.com/joeduffy/foundations-of-llms  
- **Strength:** Curated collection of essential research papers.  
- **Weakness:** Requires cross‑referencing with other resources.  
- **Time (consume):** 20–30 hours (reading all included papers once, skimming less relevant ones).  
- **Time (internalize):** 40–60 hours (take detailed notes on 5–8 core papers, replicate small experiments or derive equations).

---

### 9. Practical Deep Learning for Coders by Fast.ai
- **Type:** Online course (video + notebooks)  
- **URL:** https://course.fast.ai/  
- **Strength:** Hands‑on, top‑down approach; builds models quickly.  
- **Weakness:** Fast pace; requires foundational knowledge.  
- **Time (consume):** 20–25 hours (watch all videos at 1.5x, skim notebooks).  
- **Time (internalize):** 40–60 hours (complete all assignments, train your own small models on Colab, debug common issues).

---

### 10. The Ultimate AI Resources Repository
- **Type:** GitHub curated list  
- **URL:** https://github.com/DhanushNehru/Ultimate-AI-Resources  
- **Strength:** Massive index of links to papers, tools, communities.  
- **Weakness:** An index, not a primary learning tool.  
- **Time (consume):** 2–3 hours (browse through categories, bookmark interesting links).  
- **Time (internalize):** Ongoing – used as a reference during your 9‑month project; no fixed internalization time.

---
